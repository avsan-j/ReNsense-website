Think of your brain as a bustling city, constantly processing, creating, and managing the flow of information. To keep this city vibrant and resilient, you need to invest in its infrastructure. The equation is simple but profoundly effective: Movement + Deep Breath + Long-Distance Gaze = A Resilient and Sustainable New You.

Movement: When your body moves, your brain grooves. Physical activities such as walking, Yoga, or light exercise (even in the chair) increase blood flow to the brain, boosting cognitive function and energy levels.

Deep Breathing: Many people, often due to poor posture while using mobile devices, laptops, or even while eating, tend to lean forward, collapsing their diaphragm. This results in shallow, rapid breaths that fail to provide sufficient oxygen to the brain. Given that the lower third of the lungs absorbs two-thirds of the oxygen, engaging in deep diaphragmatic breathing is crucial.

Remember: despite the brain making up only 2% of your body mass, it demands 20% of your oxygen and nutrients.

Long-Distance Gaze: Regularly shifting your gaze from screens to distant objects reduces visual and mental fatigue. Ideally, take a moment to look at the horizon outdoors. This practice relaxes the eyes and refreshes the mind. If stepping outside isn't an option, simply sit for 10 minutes with your eyes closed, focusing on your breath, to optimize energy and enhance mental clarity.
