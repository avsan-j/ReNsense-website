Accountability

Why be accountable?

Accountability in yoga and mindset is essential for fostering personal growth and commitment to practice. It encourages practitioners to set clear intentions, stay motivated, and remain consistent in their journey. When individuals hold themselves accountable—whether through setting goals, practicing with a community, or tracking progress—they cultivate a deeper connection to their practice. This not only enhances physical and mental discipline but also promotes a supportive environment where sharing experiences can lead to collective growth and inspiration. Ultimately, accountability empowers yogis to deepen their practice and achieve a greater sense of well-being.

At ReNsense, we do our best to ensure that our clients are held accountable for their progress while learning with us because we believe consistency and dedication are key to achieving your goals while practising yoga and training your mindset. Throughout your journey into yoga and mindset, make sure you hold accountability for your actions and decisions. Make sure to track your progress and follow a clear and strict schedule when practising. You and only you are responsible for the change you bring in yourself.

Josephine, Canada. Hatha Yoga Teacher.

Hi everyone, My name is Joséphine. I was born in Zaire, raised mainly in Europe and I live in Canada since 2009. I discovered yoga 17 years ago when I was pregnant with my first son and looking for a way to relieve my sciatica pain.Yoga became an important part of my life 6 years ago. I went to Zanzibar for an Ashtanga yoga retreat and at the time, I had to make a difficult decision that would drastically change my life. That's where I met an extraordinary teacher: Dikla. She wasn't just a yoga instructor; she was a guide. Through her teaching and our conversations, Dikla made me realize that anything is possible. She showed me that yoga isn't just a physical practice but that what happens on the mat is a reflection of what happens in life.

Thanks to Dikla, I found clarity. I learned how to breathe through challenges, embrace discomfort to grow, and ground myself in my choices. That retreat gave me the courage to make that life-changing decision, and it ultimately transformed my life for the better. So today, I want to thank you, Dikla. Your teaching was a light on my path, and I'll never forget it. Thank you for sharing your wisdom and energy. You changed my life.
